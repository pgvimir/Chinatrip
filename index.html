About the Performance

Inspired by the Thai tale Suthon–Manorah — a story of longing, separation, and reconciliation — the performance moves between earth and sky, reflecting on the fragile thread that binds human devotion to the greater rhythms of nature. Harmony is not given, but patiently restored.

Structured in four movements, the work follows a cycle of imbalance and renewal. Thai traditional sonorities meet classical repertoire, newly composed music, electronics, movement, and projection. Each element resonates with the narrative, shaping a shared poetic space where tradition and innovation coexist.

Presented as part of a shared cultural dialogue, the performance draws upon philosophical sensibilities long rooted across Asia, where harmony arises through attunement rather than force.
